# Retired Claims

This document records specific claims that were made during the Quantum Meditation QPU experiment and subsequently retired due to falsification.

## Retired Claims

### Claim: Inner Register as Primary Signal Channel

**Original Statement:** "The inner register acts as the primary candidate signal channel and the middle register serves as a possible internal comparison register."

**Retirement Reason:** Per-bit decomposition and permutation testing showed no significant meditation-aligned structure in any register. Inner register patterns were either hardware artifacts (9-9 Brisbane) or statistically flat (7-11, 9-23).

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Register-Specific Temporal Reorganization

**Original Statement:** "The strongest pattern is register-specific temporal reorganization, especially in the inner register."

**Retirement Reason:** No temporal reorganization aligned with meditation windows. Apparent patterns were due to hardware settling (9-9 Brisbane) or analysis artifacts (decompression bug).

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Post-Meditation Recovery Pattern

**Original Statement:** "Post-meditation recovery behavior appears in successfully synchronized sessions."

**Retirement Reason:** No recovery pattern observed in correctly decoded data. Apparent recovery patterns were artifacts of the decompression bug or hardware settling.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Cross-Metric Correlation Sign Change

**Original Statement:** "The relationship between the entanglement proxy and interference proxy appears to change by phase (positive in control, negative in meditation)."

**Retirement Reason:** This pattern was only observed in data affected by the decompression bug. Corrected pipeline shows no significant correlation changes.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Synchronization Quality as Effect Modifier

**Original Statement:** "Delayed or asynchronous sessions do not show the same clean return-to-baseline pattern."

**Retirement Reason:** No return-to-baseline pattern exists in any session when correctly decoded. Apparent patterns were analysis artifacts.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Lead/Lag Structure

**Original Statement:** "Ordering sometimes appeared slightly before the scheduled meditation start; return-to-baseline sometimes became slower than in earlier sessions."

**Retirement Reason:** No lead/lag structure survives null testing. Apparent timing effects were hardware artifacts or analysis artifacts.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: QBism Model Parameters

**Original Statement:** "λ = 2.0 (interference suppression), γ = 1.27 (collective enhancement), τ = 10.0 (relaxation time)"

**Retirement Reason:** These parameters were fitted to data affected by the decompression bug. Corrected data shows no effects requiring such parameters.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Non-Local Consciousness Effects

**Original Statement:** "Non-local consciousness effects on quantum systems may extend beyond close proximity, as spatial separation did not diminish the constructive interference pattern."

**Retirement Reason:** The constructive interference pattern was an artifact of the decompression bug. Corrected data shows no such pattern.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

### Claim: Consciousness as Bayesian Probability Update

**Original Statement:** "Consciousness acts as a Bayesian probability update mechanism rather than causing wavefunction collapse."

**Retirement Reason:** No evidence supports this mechanism in the corrected dataset. The hypothesis was based on analysis artifacts.

**Status:** RETIRED

---

## Summary

All theoretical claims derived from the experimental data have been retired because:

1. The data they were based on was affected by a decompression bug
2. Corrected data shows no significant meditation-aligned effects
3. Apparent patterns were traced to hardware artifacts or statistical noise

No theoretical framework should be built on this dataset.
